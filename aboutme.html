About Me

Hello! I'm glad you've interested enough to learn more about me. I'm an aspiring software engineer who can apply their skills in all kinds of situations, whether that be creating a story or collaborating with a large team of developers to create a website. In 2018, I skimmed the waters of coding for the first time on Roblox's Luau. It opened the gates to the world of coding for me. There, I've trained my designer brain by creating games. In 2025, I began taking web development classes at the Center for Advanced Research and Technology (CART). This was where more of my applicable technical and core skills come from. I've developed over five website prototypes in this class, which have each respectively trained me to become the professional developer I am now. Now, I am looking to apply my skills to real industry experience. From there, I will continue to grow as a developer as I learn more and more about the intricacies of design. I love solving puzzles, and coding brings that tinkerer's mind out of me. I'm grateful to be able to share my passions with other people. Thanks for visiting.

Year In Review

I applied to the Center for Advanced Research and Technology (CART) in August 2025. Before that time, I essentially had no knowledge in any practical language. I truthfully thought, entering that classroom for the first time and getting thrown in a loop by GitHub desktop before even having touched any code, that I'd be way too under-qualified for the class, even for someone who learns as quickly as I do. It certainly wasn't easy. I stressed through months of projects and tight deadlines, all while balancing rigorous AP classes and personal projects. But with each new project came a new critical skill: AI prompt engineering, responsive design, all various APIs of JavaScript; slowly but surely, I mastered the front-stack with close attention to detail. It wasn't only my technical knowledge that grew—I also developed significantly in character. Never would sophomore me have thought that I'd be the one taking the lead in group projects on the regular; I have a natural talent for figuring things out, but never the voice to explain why we should do it the way I think. However, the pressure of group projects forced me to speak my mind—I strived to communicate with members and take initiative to push our best ideas forth. Now here I am in April 2026, nearly one year later after pushing through strenuous workloads and deadlines, with this portfolio as the proof of my learning. I'm not any less stressed than I was at the start of the year, but I've certainly leapt several levels in experience.

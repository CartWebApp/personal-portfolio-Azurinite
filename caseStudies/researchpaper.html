Research Paper

Synthesized from three sources and a personally conducted interview with a zoo specialist to propose effective ways for integrating technology into the enrichments of animals requiring greater cognitive abilities such as primates and elephants. Received a full score for flow in writing and proper citation and analysis of sources.

Concepts Applied

  • Gathering Sources: Used digital research cards for storing critical information and quotes from research papers to use when drafting my writing.
  • Source Integration/Citation: Cited sources beyond copy-pasting long paragraphs of text—paraphrased and summarized in order to keep the citation brief, improve understanding, and flow seamlessly between sentences.
  • Gathering Sources: information between sources to identify benefits of technology in enrichment, such as its ability to train natural behaviors and to automate tedious tasks such as the distribution of food around an enclosure.

Problems & Solutions

  • 1. Scheduling an Interview

    • The Problem:Me and a team reached out to animal specialists at zoos by email. The specialist originally wanted to set up a video call, but we didn't manage to schedule a proper time.
    • The Solution:We agreed that we would send our questions and receive answers via email, which allowed us to access direct quotes easily and saved us time.

  • 2. Gathering Information

    • The Problem: Research papers are long. Gathering information from them and sorting what to use in the paper proved difficult.
    • The Solution: I adopted a strategy I learnt from strategies in AVID: when reading through each source, I approached with an essential question in mind and sought information that answered it. To check for understanding, I noted any information that looked appealing in my own words.

  • 3. Citations

    • The Problem:As it turned out, I had two sources created by the same authors. Though I had written many research papers beforehand, this was an issue I never expected to have, and I didn't know how to cite them in the essay.
    • The Solution: Scouring online websites, yielded a variety of answers. I made an informed decision to use citations in the form of ([Author], “[Source]”), as I believed it made the distinction between the two sources most obvious.

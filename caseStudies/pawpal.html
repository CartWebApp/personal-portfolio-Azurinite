Concepts Learned

  • Wireframing

    I sketched out Low-Fidelity and High-Fidelity prototypes in Figma before to structure the layout for a planned coding structure.

  • Responsive Design

    I changed the layout of the app using media queries when the screen size reached certain breakpoints to seamlessly transition between mobile, tablet, and desktop viewports.

  • Semantic Structure

    I used semantic HTML elements like <nav>, <section>, and <footer> in place of elements like <div> for site accessibility and SEO.

Problems & Solutions

  • Bad Scaling / Margin

    • The Problem:Scaling images and text to fit as the screen expanded or shrunk was difficult, as we wanted to maintain a balance between readability and minimizing white space.
    • The Solution:At the time, we chose to scale text and images with vh, vw, or %. Reflecting now, however, I realize many of our sections (particularly those with images and text side by side) could have been easily scaled or laid out with CSS or Flexbox, which was something I did not have much skill in at the time.

  • 3. Inefficient User Flow

    • The Problem:The user flow was not mechanically or aesthetically satisfactory to go through: often required going through multiple web pages and/or scrolling through a vertically long page.
    • The Solution: Most user flows, such as for adding new pet profiles, were condensed into a pop-up overlay. For longer forms, such as logging new activity, I stylized the page by categorizing input fields, adding more color variety, and appending a hero image to the top of the page.

CART Showcase Reflection

CART, the facility at which I began my coding journey, hosted its annual Showcase event, in which we could visit other labs and see the work they do. Later, I reflected on what I learnt at each lab by writing a report in the form of a newspaper article… but I took a creative twist by writing from the perspective of a reporter recording what he saw in a magical castle.

Concepts Applied

  • Purposeful Language: Wrote with a particular language to convey the feeling of an ordinary reporter exploring a magical location in the medieval times
  • Detailed Imagery: Used metaphors and appealed to senses to draw vivid images in the reader's mind

Problems & Solutions

  • 1. Interpreting Language from Presenters

    • The Problem: Presenters in each lab spoke fast or used complex vocabulary; it would be difficult to understand and translate information into the reflection.
    • The Solution: An obvious answer (and one I did take) was to go to labs I knew more about, such as checking out Game Design labs given my prior experience I had in designing games. However, I also made sure to ask clarification questions at all times, such as asking to define terms or describe processes.

  • 2. Being Clear with Metaphors

    • The Problem: Since I talked about my visits as if I were literally a reporter looking at wizards performing magic, I had to somehow explain it in a way that would allow readers to understand what was actually happening.
    • The Solution: For each lab I discussed, I directly mentioned its name. I would then use exact terms that the speakers per lab used.

  • 3. Detailed Reflection

    • The Problem: I had to not get caught up in the creative twist: in some way, I had to show I was truly learning and reflecting on the experience.
    • The Solution: I made direct connections to the learning I described: for example, when I mention how the Engineering & Robotics lab showcases their machines, I connect it to how I previously (tried) to make machines with the programs they used (Arduino and Tinkercad).

Habits of Mind

  • Investigation

    Sought information from each lab and presentation I attended; took notes whenever an intriguing fact about their research or the lab was mentioned

  • Reflection

    Deliberate reflection of learning acquired from presentations in other labs—aimed for personal connection with unique diction

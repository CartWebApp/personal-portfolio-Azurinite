Problems & Solutions

  • 1. Limitations of AI Image Generation

    • The Problem:It was difficult to create images without having distorted text or unrealistic visuals.
    • The Solution:Experimenting shows that some AIs can produce more consistent results than others (Firefly produces images much faster, but ChatGPT gives limited free trials for high-quality images). Our tests proved we should mainly use Adobe Firefly for large images (e.g. hero images) and GPT sparingly for small pictures requiring high detail (e.g. images of products).

  • 2. Inconsistencies as AI developed the Next Page, and so on

    • The Problem: As ChatGPT procedurally generated one web page after another, inconsistencies began to appear. The AI would use different shades of colors compared to what it was asked to use previously. Hover animations varied between screens, and persistent images or text would scale differently per page.
    • The Solution: When issues appeared on the web page, it was difficult to tell the AI how to fix it, especially without having it cause more errors. In situations like these, humans must jump in to fix the issues AI cannot see. I directly jumped in to solve problems by changing CSS values and experimenting to see what worked. However, this was quite difficult; I elaborate upon these difficulties in the next challenge.

  • 3. Lack of CSS Knowledge at the Time

    • The Problem:Not having learnt much CSS at the time, I had difficulties understanding simple rules such as margin, aspect-ratio, and background. It had not been until later that we had learnt layout rules like flex and grid, so I could not position elements well.
    • The Solution: Consulting AI and searching on the web were vital resources to gain learning from this project. From the web, I learnt how to position with a grid (barely, but I was able to lay out profiles in a row), resize images, recolor text and elements, and change the behavior of elements when you hover over them. Others were too challenging for me to figure out at the time: for example, I had to ask AI with several prompts to help figure out why the hero image on the first page did not scale correctly—it was because two CSS rulesets were clashing.
